Research [edit]

Metabolically Coupled Dual-Strain System for Microplastic Conversion

Dec 2023 – Oct 2024 · Synthetic biology · Environmental biotechnology

This project developed a metabolically coupled dual-strain system integrating PET microplastic degradation with rhamnolipid biosynthesis, aiming to convert plastic waste into value-added products. The work involved engineering Escherichia coli for PET-degrading enzyme expression and optimizing metabolic pathways in Pseudomonas putida for conversion of degradation intermediates.

Zeng's contributions included project design and molecular biology experiments.

Multi-omics Analysis of Salt-Alkali Resistance Mechanisms in Wheat

Jun 2025 – Mar 2026 · Multi-omics · Plant stress tolerance · Bioinformatics

This research investigated salt-alkali stress responses in wheat through comparative transcriptomic analysis of the standard strain L88-31 and the salt-tolerant strain Dekang 961. The study integrated multi-omics analysis, transcription factor enrichment, STEM clustering, GWAS analysis, and single-cell marker enrichment to identify candidate genes and infer tissue-specific roles in stress tolerance.

Zeng's contributions included data analysis and data visualization.

Regulatory Mechanisms of PKS in Lipid Synthesis in Schizochytrium

Oct 2024 – May 2025 · Microbial lipid metabolism · Bioinformatics · Molecular biology

This project investigated the regulatory role of multifunctional polyketide synthase in lipid biosynthesis in Schizochytrium, with a focus on the functional significance of its modular architecture. The work applied bioinformatics analysis to the enoyl reductase domain and constructed knockout strains to evaluate its role in lipid biosynthesis.

Zeng's contributions included molecular biology experiments.

Projects [edit]

Application and Promotion of a Rapid Drug Detection System Based on Nanomaterials and AI

May 2025 – Oct 2025 · Nanomaterial sensing · AI analysis · Public-sector application

This project developed and promoted a rapid drug detection system for drug control applications. The system used nanomaterial-based sensing, AI-driven analysis, and data management to enable saliva-based testing within 10 seconds and result interpretation within 1 minute.

The system has been piloted in more than 20 cities, with over 100,000 tests conducted and 100 drug users identified. Compared with traditional methods, the detection speed and sensitivity increased by 10 times and 100 times respectively.

Zeng's contributions included business plan development, material organization, content writing, and participation in product promotion for government sectors.
